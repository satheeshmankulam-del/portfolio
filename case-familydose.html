FamilyDose —
From Figma to Play Store

How I designed and shipped a full Android medication tracker — solo, as a non-coder — using Figma for design and Claude AI for code generation. My dad kept missing his BP tablets. So I built the solution.

Role
Designer + Product Owner
Platform
Android · Google Play
Stack
Figma · Claude AI · React
Status
Live · v7.1
01 — Problem

My dad kept missing
his BP tablets.

It started personal. My father has been on daily blood pressure medication for years. Despite reminders, he'd forget doses — especially when the routine changed. Existing apps were either too complex, required cloud accounts, or weren't built for families tracking multiple people's medications.

"Every existing app assumed you were tracking your own meds. Nobody had built one for the person managing everyone else's."

That observation became the brief: build a medication tracker that works offline, requires no account, and is designed for family-level management — not just individual use.

02 — Research

Understanding what
families actually need

I audited 8 existing medication apps and spoke to family members who manage others' medications. Four clear gaps emerged across every existing solution.

🔒
Privacy over convenience
Users managing family health data strongly resist cloud sync. The trust barrier of uploading medication records to a stranger's server was the #1 reason people abandoned existing apps.
👨‍👩‍👧
One person manages many
The key user isn't the patient — it's the family member who tracks everyone else. Apps designed for self-tracking create friction when the user is managing their parents, spouse, and child simultaneously.
🔔
Reminders that actually fire
On aggressive battery-management devices (Samsung, Xiaomi, OnePlus), background alarms are frequently killed. This makes medication reminders unreliable on the exact phones most used in India.
📱
Zero friction to start
Every onboarding screen between install and first use is a dropout risk. The app needs to get users to their first medication entry in under 60 seconds — no tutorials, no account creation.
03 — Design Process

Figma to Play Store —
the full journey

01
Information architecture in Figma
Mapped the core flows: add medication → set schedule → view today's doses → mark as taken → history. Designed 40+ screens including dark mode, empty states, error states, and all edge cases before writing a single line of code.
02
Family-first data model
Structured the data model around family members as the top-level entity, not medications. Each member has their own medication list, schedule, and history. Filters and views are member-scoped by default.
03
AI-assisted development with Claude
Described each screen and interaction in plain English to Claude. Iterated on the generated React JSX until every screen matched the Figma designs. No formal coding knowledge — just clear UX thinking translated into working code.
04
Real device testing across Android variants
Tested specifically on Samsung S23 Ultra, OnePlus, and Xiaomi — notorious for killing background processes. Fixed alarm persistence for all three by implementing foreground service notifications and battery exemption prompts.
05
Google Play submission and iteration
Passed Play Store review on the first attempt. Now at v7.1 (versionCode 13) with 13+ features including PIN lock, JSON export/import, trilingual support (English, Malayalam, Hindi), image lightbox, and a 100-entry cap with compression.
